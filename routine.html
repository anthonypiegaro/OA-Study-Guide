What a Warmup Should Accomplish

A warm-up should be a functional, comprehensive, and somewhat difficult, causing the athlete to focus throughout the warm-up.

Activation

Integrate specific movements to "turn on" the muscles responsible for joint stabilization and protection, which not only make the athlete's workout safer, but creates a more efficient one as well

Self-myofascial release (SMR) is a method that helps reduce adhesions in the muscle tissue and allows your muscles to relax.

There are two great tools for SMF:

  • Foam Roller: Great for body parts with large surface areas
  • Lacrosse Ball: Great for more concentrated areas that are too difficult for the foam roller to reach

Muscles are surrounded by a film of connective tissue called facia. When the muscle is overused, small knots may develope in the connective tissue, creating the potential for a decrease in the efficiency of a muscle to perform a particular task.

Self-myofascial release takes advantage of the body's Golgi tendon organs, which are mechonoreceptors in the body that are responseable for autoregulating tension. Once stimulated, like by using one of the techniques above, they help the tissue relax, lossening up the fascial knots.

This in turn may increase the efficieny of muscle tissue to perform a particular task.
